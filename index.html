Education Research

Exploring the Experiences of English Major Teachers Handling Non-English Subjects

This study explored the lived experiences of English major teachers assigned to teach non-English subjects in Danao City using a qualitative phenomenological design. Six English teachers from two public schools were purposively selected and interviewed in depth, with data analyzed through Colaizzi’s method to identify recurring themes. Findings revealed major challenges, such as difficulties in mastering unfamiliar content, language barriers, instructional shifts, and the psychological toll of out-of-field teaching. Teachers coped through self-directed learning, peer collaboration, professional development, and mindset adjustments, with administrative support, differentiated instruction, and student centered strategies helping to ease difficulties. The study emphasizes the need for targeted training and institutional support to better prepare English majors for teaching non specialized subjects and suggests future research on the long-term effects on teacher performance, satisfaction, and student outcomes.
